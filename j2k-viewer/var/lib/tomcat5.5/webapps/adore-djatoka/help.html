Image Viewer Help

Image Navigation

Zooming
To zoom in or out while viewing the image, click the and buttons. You can also use your mouse's scroll wheel.
If you do not see these buttons, then the particular image you are viewing does not support zooming.
Panning Image
You can drag the image using your mouse. Additionally, you can drag the view box inside the image thumbnail located at the top right corner of the image viewer. Lastly, you can use your arrow keys to move the image around.
Printing
Press in order to print the image. You will print the portion of the image you are currently viewing.
Saving
You save a copy of the image by pressing . This will enable you to download the raw jpeg2000 file. You will be required to pass a captcha in order to download the image.

More Info

Megapixel images are difficult to display online. The images in our collections often reach sizes over 100MB and are highly detailed, which is difficult to deliver with standard image formats. We wanted to provide a rich browsing environment that let users zoom in and out to see greater detail and simply click and drag to view more of the image, similar in functionality to Google Maps.

We encode our archival TIF images into JPEG2000 (and previously MrSID) format and place those files on our web server. As you interact with our images, JavaScripts running on your browser send instructions about what zoom level and area you'd like to view to Adore-Djatoka, also installed on our server. This technique is commonly known as Ajax, or Asynchronous JavaScript and XML, although technically we are not using the XMLHttpRequest object to interchange data.
